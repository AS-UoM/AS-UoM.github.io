Research Interests and Career Objectives

Humanoid Robotics

My main interest lies in humanoid robotics, the operation of humanoid robots and how they could be used in supportive and consumer roles. My goal is to produce my own humanoid robot as shown in the 'Personal Projects' section and to work in the humanoid robotics sector.

3D modelling

3D modelling is another interest of mine, linking to robotics and allowing the freedom to design a range of different devices for projects.

PCB Design

I aim to continue developing different printed circuit board designs using digital and mixed signal components to produce effective solutions to less explored areas of circuit design as shown in my PCB project.

Microcontroller Programming and Machine Learning

I aim to develop machine learning models for use in small-power applications, to make machine learning more accessible on platforms with less processing power.

University Projects and Videos

BEng Mechatronics PCB Project

My BEng third year project focused on the development of a bespoke printed circuit board designed to provide a platform to compare mixed signal crosstalk performance between multiple different ground plane layer designs. It included the creation of a suitable circuit diagram and schematic, implementing digital, analogue and mixed signal electrical components in a PCB layout. The project also developed electrical testing skills and provided experience developing a PCB design through an iterative approach. This project was designed to provide quantitative evidence on an area of study with less published work to be able to contribute to the qualitative evidence already available.

Research on Robotics Systems for Sustainable Development

As part of my MSc course, I created a video exploring the use of robotic systems with regards to sustainability, the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals and ethical use. It focused on the classification of robots as according to the International Federation of Robotics, explaining the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals and how robots are used across them, finishing with a study on a specific use-case of robotics in terms of a specific sustainable development goal (SDG 2).

Research on Robots in Ethically Aligned Design (EAD)

This research explored the ethical use and impact of autonomous and intelligent systems (A/IS), researching Ethically Aligned Design as a concept and its importance. It further analyses ethical design through research on diverse ethical practices and how those values could be embedded into autonomous and intelligent systems to improve the ethical impaxt of autonomous systems.

BEng Group Project - Line-following Semi-autonomous Robot

As part of the second year of my BEng Mechatronics course, me and and my second year group members created and programmed a semi-autonomous mobile robot designed to follow a series of straight and curved lines using optical sensors. This robot was tasked with the ability to follow a white line on a black track with any unseen track shape, completing multiple demonstrations over the project's lifespan, ending with a final demonstration. Alongside the robot, sensor and motor characterisation research was taken, with multiple report being created based on the robot.

Research and Experiments on Control of Wheeled Robots using MATLAB and Simulink

During this research, open-loop and closed-loop control methods were implemented in a simulation of a differential drive robot represented in 2D space. This research recorded the position and trajectory of the simulated robot over a period of time, allowing for comparisons between results and the explanation of the benefits of closed-loop control in a robotic system.
